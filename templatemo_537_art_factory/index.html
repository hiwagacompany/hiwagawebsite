About Us

Our Vision

In the future, we plan to create a better cotton, to improve sustainability and make a mark on climate change.

  • Decreasing Water Usage

    By increasing the levels of the photosynthetic protein (PsbS), the plant will partially close it's stomata to conserve at least 25% more water.

  • Durability

    Additionally, we plan to edit repressors found in two different proteins in cellulose, to strengthen the plant.

  • Testing it Out

    Trials outsource farmers to raise our cotton until fully grown. Growth rate, cotton's texture, and other relating factors are monitored. More Info.

Frequently Asked Questions

Our company wants to be as transparent as we can. Doing that means answering pressing questions. Here it is.

How much would this cost?

Based on the research gathered, the cotton seeds would cost approximately $50 per hectare. This value is projected to decrease over the years.

We want to make sure that our cotton is accessible to all, and we're going to actively try to accomplish that mission.

What type of cotton is being edited?

We are focusing on reducing upland cotton, which currently takes up 90% of the global cotton production.

Upland cotton is widely used in the fashion industry - so to have a bigger impact, we've decided to focus on that first.

What areas are we currently targeting?

As of right now, we plan to target cotton farmers in the North America - like Texas.

Hopefully in the future, we can further reach other areas, to make sustainable cotton more accessible for everyone.

Why did you decide to go with cotton?

Cotton is a plant that uses enormous amounts of water simply to grow, yet it's still so widely used in the fashion industry. We know that it's hard for people to change their habits when it's been the same for decades.

So we decided to change the seed.

What does Hiwaga mean?

Hiwaga is a word in Tagalog that means mystery and full of wonder. We wanted to signify that there's so much we don't know - and how willing we are to explore the unknown.

What are you going to do next?

Our next steps would be to test out our hypothesis relating to increasing cotton's sustainability. We plan to use contract labs in order to have these trials done.

The process would take between a few years, to ensure efficiency and maximum effectiveness.
